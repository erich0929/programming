Title :

TUI Travel's flying after posting 21% jump in third-quarter profits

Tour operator on track to meet annual profit growth goal as merger talks with German majority owner continue

Contributor :

Public Date :

Friday 8 August 2014 11.52 BST

Content :

TUI Travel, Europe's biggest tour operator by revenues, looks on track to meet its annual profit growth goal after a 21% jump in third-quarter profits, powered by sales of higher-margin holidays.

It is in talks with German-based TUI AG – which owns 55% of the British firm – regarding a £4.4bn merger deal that would create the world's largest leisure tourism group. The companies have until 19 September to announce a formal proposal.

TUI Travel is targeting 7-10% growth in underlying operating profit for the 12 months to the end of September, but takeover rules prevented it from re-affirming guidance on Friday.

"I can't comment in terms of a forecast for this year because we're in an offer period and I've got restrictions in terms of the takeover code," its chief executive, Peter Long, said. He added that there was "no business reason" for the absence of guidance in the statement.

TUI Travel and TUI AG announced a plan to merge in an all-share, nil-premium deal in June. Investors had long expected such a tie-up since TUI Travel was created in 2007 from the merger of Britain's First Choice and the travel business of TUI AG.

The two firms last held merger talks in 2013 but a deal collapsed after TUI AG said an offer would not make sense given their share prices at the time.

Analysts at the corporate advice firm Numis said the current merger plan did not look attractive for independent TUI Travel shareholders, given the underperformance of TUI AG's share price, and that meant either a more favourable deal could emerge or that the merger could be scrapped. "Together with today's encouraging trading update, this makes us more positive on TUI Travel," Wyn Ellis of Numis said.

Shares in TUI AG have lost 14% of their value since the day before the merger talks were announced, underperforming TUI Travel's stock which is trading 9% lower.

TUI Travel shares traded down 0.8% to 352.75p, slightly lagging behind Britain's bluechip index which was down 0.7%.

Long said that TUI Travel's strong performance was being driven by its "unique" holiday concept.

These higher-margin holidays accounted for 71% of summer bookings this year and allow TUI Travel to differentiate itself from competitors who sell more commodity-like holidays, Long explained, adding that this was a particular advantage given that the market this year was more competitive than last.

TUI Travel on Friday reported underlying operating profit on a like-for-like basis of £92m in the three months to 30 June compared with £76m in the same period last year.
