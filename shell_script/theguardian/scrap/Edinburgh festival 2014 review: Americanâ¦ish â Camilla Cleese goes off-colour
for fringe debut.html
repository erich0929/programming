Title :

Edinburgh festival 2014 review: American…ish – Camilla Cleese goes off-colour for fringe debut

Gilded Balloon, Edinburgh
With digs at her famous dad, Cleese's LA gold-digger persona stays provocatively on the right side of caricature

Contributor :

Brian Logan

Public Date :

Friday 8 August 2014 15.54 BST

Content :

"Everyone is expecting me to fail," Camilla Cleese said ahead of her Edinburgh fringe debut. A bit strong, but there's no denying that dad John casts a long shadow. Tonight, performing 20 minutes as part of a three-hander US comedy bill, she confounds the doubters. There's barely time to make a significant mark, but she showcases a playfully black-hearted persona and exudes a confidence that suggests she won't readily be eclipsed by anyone – not even daddy.

There are gags about her famous father, of course – but she has the self-possession to make us wait for them. First she jokes about her height – she's 6ft-plus – and the sins committed by small men, including (when his blades are removed) Oscar Pistorius. Yes, she's off-colour, like the material about adopting an African child, but Cleese carries it off with an amoral, LA gold-digger persona that stays provocatively on the right side of caricature.

Then come the digs at dad, which revolve around his endless cycle of marriages and divorces. "We have a new child in the family," she announces, then after a pause: "my new stepmother". Cue jokes about having a "mommy" who's not much older than she is – tart jabs with a distinct many-a-true-word air about them.

It's an enjoyable set, and her co-stars just about maintain the standard. OK, so Cort McCown frays the patience with sub-Doug Stanhope gender politics and his call for disciplinarian parenting. But he's a capable old-school club comic who manages to find new humour (who knew there was any left?) in material about Ryanair. And Sarah Tiana showcases a teasing set about being single, 36 and from the deep south. There's some way to go before UK audiences see Cleese as anything other than Basil Fawlty's daughter. But her fringe debut represents a decent start.

• Until 13 August. Box office: 0131-226 1000. Venue: Gilded Balloon, Edinburgh.
